### Research Question 1
**Which age group is most affected by air pollution-related health outcomes — and how does this vary by city?**

#### **Source** 
- The Lancet Regional Health - https://www.thelancet.com/journals/lansea/article/PIIS2772-3682(22)00085-3/fulltext
- The Hindu - https://www.thehindu.com/sci-tech/health/death-rates-from-air-pollution-spikes-vary-across-cities-study/article68363334.ece#:~:text=Explained%20%7C%20How%20can%20India%20monitor,period%2C%20Bengaluru%20had%202%2C102%20deaths.

#### Findings
- Children under the age of 5 and elderly (60+ years) are the age groups most significantly affected by air pollution-related health outcomes. Recent 2024-2025 data indicated that children under 5 face a disproportionately high risk, with air pollution linked to over 7 lakh deaths in this group.

**Variation by City**
- **Delhi(NCR)** - Highest mortality burden in India, with 11.5% of annual deaths linked to air pollution.Children here face a **permanent decline** in lung function.
- **Ahmedabad** - Significant respiratory admissions for children under 6 throughout the year, peaking in winter and summer.
**UP & Bihar** - High prevalence of neonatal mortality(NMR) and infant mortality(IMR) due to widespread use of solid cooking fuels.


#### Business Implications
>- AirPure's market should target the customer segment of age group **28-40** purchasing for children and adult children purchasing for elderly parents are the highest-urgency segments.
>- Product features should reflect this : **quiet sleep mode** for children's bedrooms, simple **one-button operation** for elderly users, and a mobile app for remote monitoring by caregivers.

### Research Question 3
**What is the relationship between a city’s population size and its average AQI — do larger 
cities always suffer from worse air quality? (Consider 2024 population and AQI data for 
this)**

#### Data Limitation - 
There is no accurate city-wise population data available for the year 2024 which makes it impossible to get accurate insights using the proxy data(which either contains urban population data or the data of only large cities by population). However, certain articles have stated that "There is a positive correlation between the city's population and its air quality (e.g. Delhi)"

**Conclusion** :
But population is not the only factor affecting the city's air quality. Biomass and crop burning in other states(Punjab/Haryana),industrial activities and geographic and climate factors etc. all affect the air quality in different manner.

Population size is a useful market sizing proxy but a poor predictor of pollution severity.High population + high AQI must both be present to identify a priority market.


#### Business Implications
>1.  Prioritize states/cities where there is a visible increase in AQI along with a large population spread, providing a large consumer market for the AirPure Purifiers.
>2. **Bad air quality is not a premium problem - it is everybody's problem.** Launch purifiers that target each consumer segment serving all income levels . A basic essentials tier for price-sensitive markets in UP/Bihar, a mid-range Pro tier for metro professionals, and a premium elite tier for high-income urban households in Delhi and Bengaluru. A single price point leaves the majority of the addressable market unreached.

### Research Question 4
**How aware are Indian citizens of what AQI (Air Quality Index) means — and do they 
understand its health implications? **

#### **Source** - https://asar.co.in/report/perception-study-on-air-quality-in-17-cities/

#### Findings
Air Pollution Awareness Study
17 Indian Cities | n=5000 respondents

- **Awareness** - **95%** are aware of air pollution yet only **35%** understand AQI and PM2.5/PM10 knowledge remains critically low, particularly among less-educated respondents.
- **Health Impact** - 93% acknowledge pollution's negative effects, 85% report feeling physically ill on poor air quality days.
- **Awareness-action gap** - with 95% awareness but only 13% taking active steps, the majority of Indians know pollution is harming them but have not translated that concern into protective behaviour.
- Only **43%** wear masks and **27%** take absolutely no precautions despite near-universal awareness.

#### Business Implications
> **The market does not need more awareness - it needs a reason to act.** AirPure's market should bridge the awareness-action gap by making clean air feel immediately accessible .
>
> Target audience who are taking active steps - they are the readiest buyers. 
> Use TV and radio as they are right mass awareness channels - not digital alone.71% respondents cite TV and radio as their primary pollution information source, yet most D2C brands in this space rely heavily on Instagram and performance marketing. Airpure has an opportunity to reach a broader and older demographic - including the caregivers of children and elderly parents through, regional TV ads during peak pollution months of **Oct to Jan** when pollution is top of mind and purchase intent is highest.


### Research Question 5
**Which pollution control policies introduced by the Indian government in the past 5 years 
have had the most measurable impact on improving air quality — and how have these 
impacts varied across regions or cities?**

#### **Source**
- https://prana.cpcb.gov.in/#/NCAPTracker
- https://www.pib.gov.in/PressReleasePage.aspx?PRID=2147751&reg=3&lang=2
- https://prana.cpcb.gov.in/ncapDashboard/download_public_portal_file/17_IC_MoM_10_10_2024_1730267081004586.pdf
- https://cwejournal.org/vol15no1/an-analysis-of-grap-task-force-directions-for-improved-aqi-in-delhi-during-2018
- https://caqm.nic.in/index1.aspx?lsid=4168&lev=2&lid=4171&langid=1#:~:text=Order%20dated%2002.04.2025%20%2D%20Implementation,schedule%20of%20GRAP%20in%20Delhi%2DNCR

#### Findings
- **National Clean Air Programme(NCAP) - 2019 onwards**
 The Indian government launched the NCAP in 2019 with a target to reduce PM concentration by 20-30% by 2024 in 102 non-attainment cities nationwide. 
 
**Impact**- 8.8% per-year decrease of PM2.5 pollution in six non-attainment cities from 2017 through 2022.By 2022, four of these cities met the NCAP target and achieved over 20% reduction relative to 2017 levels.
 However, 44 out of 57 non-attainment cities with continuous monitoring data still failed to national standard in 2022.

- **GRAP(Graded Response Action Plan)** - GRAP uses air quality modelling and forecasts to automatically trigger actions in advance rather than waiting for pollution to peak.

**Impact**-Between Jan and Aug 2024 , Delhi's average AQI was recorded at 172- a significant improvement compared to 187 in 2023, 194 in 2022. This period did not record a single "Severe" AQI day.

- **BS6 Emisson standards and Vehicular Controls** - Cities tightened control over vehicular pollution through strict PUC enforcement and the "no-PUC, no-fuel"rule, especially in Delhi.

**Impact**- The government promoted a shift towards CNG and electric buses in Delhi's public transport fleet , significantly cutting vehicular emissions which contribute up to 40% of Delhi's winter pollution.


#### Business Implications

>The policy trajectory confirms that India's air quality problem will improve gradually over decades- not years-sustaining long-term structural demand for indoor air purification solutions. AirPure's market is not threatened by government action- it is validated by its insufficiency.
